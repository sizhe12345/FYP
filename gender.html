A county (Malay: Daerah; called "Jajahan" in the state of Kelantan) is a category of administrative districts in Malaysia, under the status of states. A county is administered by the County Office and the Land Office (eg Petaling County is administered by the Petaling County Office and the Land Office). In West Malaysia, counties are administrative districts under the state and belong to sub-administrative districts. Under the county, it is divided into townships, also known as "wujin" or "districts". The townships are of little administrative importance and are mainly used for land management and title deeds. In East Malaysia, a county is an administrative region under a province and belongs to the third-level administrative region. For example, Tuaran is a district under Sabah Coast Province. Usually the name of the county will be the same as the name of the town, such as Sandakan District and Sandakan. In Sarawak, there is also a deputy county, which is higher than the local district (Land District) and lower than the county, while there is no Wujin in Sabah. Due to its small size, Perlis is the only state in Malaysia that does not have a county. Counties and local governments in Malaysia are different. For example, there are three local governments in Petaling Jaya, Subang Jaya, Shah Alam and Petaling Jaya. In Penang, the status of counties is lower than that of local governments. For example, there are three counties in North, Central and South under the City Hall of Seberang Perai Province.

Overall, there were more males than females. In 2020, male population outnumbered the female with 16.8 million (males) and 15.9 million (females). The sex ratio for as a whole nation remained at 106 males per 100 females. The sex ratio of citizens is slightly decreased from for a five-year period with only 103 (males) : 100 (females) between 2010 till 2014 and 102 (males) : 100 (females) for a period from 2015-2020.
